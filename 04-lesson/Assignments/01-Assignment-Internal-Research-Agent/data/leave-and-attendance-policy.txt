Document Title: Leave and Attendance Policy

1. Purpose
This policy explains the company’s expectations regarding working hours, attendance, punctuality, planned leave, sick leave, and unplanned absences. The purpose of this policy is to ensure fair treatment of employees while maintaining business continuity and proper team coordination.

2. Scope
This policy applies to all full-time, part-time, probationary, and contract employees unless a separate employment agreement states otherwise.

3. Working Hours
The standard working schedule is Monday to Friday, from 9:30 AM to 6:30 PM, with a one-hour meal break. Employees are expected to be available during core business hours unless they have manager-approved flexible scheduling.

4. Attendance Expectations
Employees must report to work on time and be ready to begin assigned duties at the scheduled start time. Regular attendance is considered an essential job responsibility. Frequent lateness, early departures, or absence without notice may affect performance evaluation and may result in corrective action.

5. Late Arrival
If an employee expects to arrive late, the employee must inform the reporting manager as early as possible, preferably before the start of the shift. Occasional lateness may be excused for valid reasons, but repeated lateness without sufficient cause may lead to verbal warning, written warning, or further disciplinary review.

6. Planned Leave
Employees should submit planned leave requests through the company leave management system at least 5 working days in advance. Longer leave periods, including vacation of more than 3 consecutive working days, should be requested at least 2 weeks in advance whenever possible. Approval depends on project requirements, staffing availability, and manager review.

7. Sick Leave
Employees who are unwell and unable to work must notify their reporting manager or team lead as early as possible on the same day. If sick leave extends beyond 2 consecutive working days, the company may request medical documentation. Abuse of sick leave may lead to review by HR.

8. Casual and Emergency Leave
In emergency situations, employees should notify the manager as soon as reasonably possible. HR and the reporting manager will review the situation and determine whether the leave will be treated as approved emergency leave, unpaid leave, or another leave category.

9. Unapproved Absence
Failure to report to work without notifying the manager may be treated as unapproved absence. Repeated unapproved absence may result in salary deduction, disciplinary action, or escalation under the employee conduct policy.

10. Leave Without Pay
If an employee has exhausted available leave balances, additional time off may be granted as leave without pay at the discretion of management and HR. Approval is not automatic and will depend on business needs and the employee’s circumstances.

11. Public Holidays
The company will publish an annual holiday calendar. Employees are expected to follow the official holiday schedule applicable to their work location unless their role requires alternative scheduling.

12. Attendance During Remote Work
Employees working remotely are still expected to remain available during assigned working hours, attend scheduled meetings, respond to official communication within a reasonable time, and maintain productivity standards similar to office-based work.

13. Time Tracking and Records
Employees may be required to log attendance, work hours, or leave through company-approved systems. Falsification of attendance or leave records is considered misconduct and may result in disciplinary action.

14. Manager Responsibility
Managers are responsible for reviewing leave requests fairly, monitoring attendance concerns, and escalating repeated issues to HR. Managers must ensure business continuity while avoiding bias or inconsistency in leave decisions.

15. Policy Violations
Violation of this policy may lead to counseling, warning, salary adjustment where permitted, or disciplinary action depending on severity and frequency.

16. Contact
Employees with questions about leave balances, attendance records, or approval rules should contact HR or their reporting manager.
